The Setup Process: Step-by-Step

This tab is for international investors—foreign individuals (any nationality; a U.S. citizen founder is a common example) or a foreign parent company—setting up a WFOE—a wholly foreign-owned enterprise (外商独资企业), the usual mainland vehicle for foreign investment. Work the steps in order; optional detail is folded below. Budget registered capital (steps 8–9) and hosting cost before you file.

4

Show or hide additional detail for this step

The tenant may not exist on paper yet; after incorporation and chops, the WFOE may reimburse allowable pre-establishment costs—confirm with your agent and accountant.

5

Retain a corporate services firm (WFOE registration agent) with a written scope—AMR filing, chops, and optional bank / bookkeeping support.

Show or hide additional detail for this step
  • Chinese law does not universally require a foreign founder to use an agent, but AMR online portals, templates, and local practice are almost entirely in Chinese—most overseas investors hire a firm to draft articles, run the AMR package, and coordinate chops and bank KYC.
  • Compare quotes before you finalize notarized / apostille packs: “incorporation only” vs. full-service (bank onboarding, bookkeeping, annual compliance).
  • Government AMR filing charges are usually modest; the main cash outlay here is professional service fees.

6

Notarize your passport or parent-company documents with a notary in your home country (e.g. a U.S. Notary Public is typical for U.S. passport holders).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Individual investor = passport; corporate parent = entity docs—your agent will list exactly what is needed.

7

Obtain an Apostille from your country’s designated Hague authority (e.g. a U.S. state Secretary of State for documents originating in the United States).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

You no longer need Chinese Embassy legalization for this path—the Apostille is the standard certification.

8

Set registered capital and pay-in schedule in the articles—do not wire equity yet.

Show or hide additional detail for this step
  • Registered capital (注册资本) is the subscribed total on the license; it is not the same as annual rent or payroll.
  • Under PRC Company Law (revised 1 Jul 2024), full pay-in within five years of establishment unless transition rules apply for older companies.
  • Choose amount using: minimum viable for your sector, business plan, license minimums, banking credibility, and repatriation plans—align with your accountant.
  • Employer social insurance + housing fund is separate from this line—see step 9 and the dashboard.

9

Model a 12-month budget: rent, agents, payroll, and statutory “five insurances + fund.”

Show or hide additional detail for this step
  • Pre-WFOE costs (lease, travel) often come from personal or parent funds; reimbursement after setup may be possible—confirm with your agent.
  • Payroll + statutory is usually the largest variable for a CAD shop—use the dashboard.
  • Assume onshore operating cash in China after capital injection (steps 17–18) and revenue.

10

Name Legal Rep, Executive Director, and Supervisor—two roles cannot be the same person.

Show or hide additional detail for this step

The Supervisor cannot be the Legal Representative or Director. These names go into articles and bank mandates.

11

File the incorporation package with the AMR (lease + apostille + articles).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Submit to the local Administration for Market Regulation (AMR): lease, apostilled identity/parent docs, articles (capital, pay-in schedule, roles), and other required company rules.

12

Collect your Business License (营业执照)—the company legally exists.

Show or hide additional detail for this step

You still have no operational corporate bank accounts until steps 14–15.

13

Register and carve your company chops (official + financial seals).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

PSB + licensed vendor. A red chop on a contract is often decisive—secure storage; whoever holds chops controls signing.

14

Open the RMB basic deposit account (基本户) with license + chops.

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Main RMB account for salaries, rent, and tax—often the only account that can pay cash salaries. KYC applies.

15

Open the foreign-investment capital account for inbound equity (FCY).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Name varies by bank (FDI / capital account). Pairs with SAFE registration of the foreign-invested enterprise.

Prepare the bank’s capital-contribution / FX forms with your agent (投资款 wording).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

The remittance must be coded as registered capital—not an informal “transfer.” Codes differ by bank.

Wire registered capital from abroad in foreign currency to the designated inbound account.

Bank records paid-in capital (实缴) toward SAFE filing; obtain FX settlement documents. Multiple tranches allowed within five-year pay-in (step 8).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Follow the bank’s process; this is company capital settlement, not personal remittance.

You need this to bill customers compliantly on the mainland.

Register for social insurance—then you can hire and run payroll.

Mandatory contributions are paid from the RMB basic account, funded by capital (steps 17–18) and revenue. Compare cities on the dashboard.

Decide shareholders, legal representative, supervisor, and (where required) finance contact; prepare PRC ID cards and mobile numbers for real-name verification.

Director and supervisor cannot be the same person in the standard one-shareholder layout; your filing portal will flag conflicts.

2

Reserve 3–5 candidate names and secure a compliant registered address—lease + property certificate, or (where permitted) a government/business-park cluster address.

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Many cities run name checks inside the same “one-stop” website; the AMR must approve the final name before issuance.

3

Set subscribed capital, shareholding ratios, payment timeline, and business scope (and any license-triggering lines).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Under the 2024 Company Law, subscribed amounts must generally be fully paid within five years of incorporation unless transition rules apply—document the schedule in the articles.

4

File online through the provincial government service portal / enterprise “one-stop” (e.g. 开办企业一窗通)—upload IDs, address proof, generated articles, and e-sign with facial recognition.

AMR review → receive the 营业执照 (electronic license immediate in many cities; paper optional).

Show or hide additional detail for this step

Carve and file official seals (公章, 财务章, 法人章, 发票章 as needed) with the public-security filing system.

Open the RMB basic bank account (基本户) with the license and seals—KYC is lighter than a WFOE but still in-person or video per bank policy.

Complete tax registration and enable digital invoices (数电票) with the tax bureau—often linked through the one-stop flow.

Register for social insurance and housing fund accounts so you can hire and run payroll compliantly.

Because Hong Kong and Macau operate under separate legal systems, the setup process is entirely different and vastly simplified compared to the mainland WFOE.

🇭🇰

Hong Kong Setup

  1. Hire a Company Secretary: By law, you must hire a local HK individual or agency to act as your Company Secretary and provide a registered office address.
  2. Provide KYC Docs: Give the agency a copy of your passport and a recent utility bill (proof of address).
  3. File Online: The agency will file Form NNC1 (Incorporation Form) with the Companies Registry.
  4. Receive Certificate: The company is usually incorporated within 24 to 48 hours. No "chops" or complex government visits are required.
  5. Bank Account (The Hard Part): Opening a corporate bank account in HK is notoriously strict due to anti-money laundering laws. Expect this to take weeks and require an in-person visit or video interview.
🇲🇱

Macau Setup

  1. Name Approval: Submit an application to the Macau Commercial and Movable Property Registry (CRCBM) to reserve a company name.
  2. Draft Articles: Prepare the Memorandum and Articles of Association. Most foreign businesses form a "Limitada" (Lda.).
  3. Notary Signature: The founders must sign the incorporation documents in front of a Macanese Notary Public.
  4. Registration: File the notarized documents with the CRCBM.
  5. Tax Bureau: Submit a Declaration of Commencement of Operations to the Macau Finance Services Bureau. Total time: roughly 2 to 3 weeks.

Hainan Free Trade Port (Island)

Show or hide Hainan Free Trade Port policy context Special about Hainan

The whole province operates as the Hainan Free Trade Port. Under current rules, “special customs operations” apply island-wide: many goods imported from abroad enter with zero import duties, VAT, and consumption tax (with mainland-bound shipments generally taxed at the “second line” unless substantially transformed on-island). This is aimed at tourism, modern services, high-tech, healthcare, and logistics.

  • Corporate income tax: 15% for qualifying enterprises in encouraged industries with substantive operations (policy extended in phases under current central notices; verify end dates with counsel).
  • Personal income tax: Eligible high-end and urgently-needed talent may cap the effective burden at 15% on qualifying Hainan-sourced income (rules for residency and recognition apply; confirm active frameworks with counsel).
