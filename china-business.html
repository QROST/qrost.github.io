The Setup Process (Mainland vs. SAR)

This section outlines the legal framework for establishing your business entity. While mainland China utilizes the WFOE structure, Special Administrative Regions (SARs) like Hong Kong and Macau operate under distinct common law or civil law frameworks, drastically altering the setup and tax timeline.

Mainland WFOE Standard Process

Applies to cities like Shanghai, Shenzhen, Beijing, Chengdu, etc.

  • Weeks 1-3: Pre-Licensing & Lease Signing.
  • Weeks 3-6: Document Legalization (Apostille of US documents).
  • Weeks 6-8: Business License Application via AMR.
  • Weeks 8-10: Carving of official Company Chops (Seals).
  • Weeks 10-12: Tax, Bank, and Social Security Registration.

SAR Setup: Hong Kong & Macau

SARs do not use the WFOE structure. They are separate customs and tax territories.

  • Hong Kong: Setup is incredibly fast (often 1-2 weeks). Form a Private Limited Company via the Companies Registry. English is an official legal language. No "Chop" system required for basic validity.
  • Macau: Form a "Limitada" (Lda.). Requires interactions with the Commercial Registry. Portuguese and Chinese are official.
  • Labor Law Distinction: Neither uses the heavy mainland Social Insurance system. HK uses MPF (Mandatory Provident Fund, capped low). Macau uses a very low fixed-rate social security contribution.
