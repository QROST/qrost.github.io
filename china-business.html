Phase 1: Pre-Licensing & Planning

Before applying, you must define the company's structure and secure a physical location. Virtual offices are generally not permitted for WFOEs.

  • Determine Business Scope: Must be explicitly defined (e.g., "Engineering Design Services", "CAD Drafting", "Technology Consulting"). You can only issue invoices for services listed here.
  • Registered Capital: While statutory minimums have been abolished, you must declare an amount sufficient to cover initial operations (e.g., $50,000 - $100,000 USD). This money must actually be injected over a stated period.
  • Lease Agreement: You must sign a commercial lease. The landlord must provide specific property ownership certificates.
  • Name Approval: Submit proposed Chinese names to the local Administration for Market Regulation (AMR).

Phase 2: Document Legalization

Chinese authorities require proof of the foreign investor's identity and standing, which must be authenticated.

  • If investing as an Individual (US Citizen): Your US Passport must be notarized, authenticated by the state, and then legalized by the Chinese Embassy/Consulate in the US.
  • If investing via a US LLC/Corp: Certificate of Incorporation, Articles of Organization, and a Bank Reference Letter must go through the same notarization and Chinese consular legalization process.
  • Note for 2025/2026: With the Apostille Convention in effect for China, the process is streamlined compared to previous years, often bypassing the final consular legalization step if the US state issues an Apostille.

Phase 3: Business License Application

This is the formal registration with the government. Most applications are now processed through a "Five-in-One" online portal.

  • Submit Articles of Association, Feasibility Study, Lease Agreement, Legalized IDs, and forms appointing the Legal Representative, Executive Director, and Supervisor.
  • The Legal Representative: A crucial role with significant legal power and liability. Does not need to reside in China or be Chinese, but holds the primary responsibility for the company.
  • Upon approval, the Administration for Market Regulation (AMR) issues the physical Business License. The company officially exists.

Phase 4: Post-Licensing & Company Chops

In China, physical stamps (chops) hold more legal weight than a signature.

  • Register with the Public Security Bureau (PSB) to carve the official company chops.
  • Required Chops:
    • Official Company Chop (for binding contracts)
    • Financial Chop (for banking)
    • Legal Representative Chop (personal stamp of the LR)
    • Invoice (Fapiao) Chop (for tax receipts)
  • Security of these chops is paramount; whoever holds them controls the company.

Phase 5: Bank Accounts & Tax Registration

Finalizing the operational setup allows you to inject capital, pay employees, and issue invoices.

  • Open Bank Accounts:
    • RMB Basic Account: For daily operations, payroll, and local tax payments.
    • Foreign Currency Capital Account: Required to receive the Registered Capital from the US investor.
  • Tax Registration: Register with the State Taxation Administration. Apply for General Taxpayer status if required, and obtain the system to issue "Fapiao" (official VAT invoices).
  • Social Security Registration: Open accounts with the local housing fund and social security bureaus to process employee benefits.

Employment & Hiring Guide

Navigating Chinese labor law requires precision. The legal framework heavily protects employees, making proper documentation crucial.

📄

Written Contracts

A written employment contract must be signed within one month of the start date. Failure to do so requires the employer to pay double salary for up to 11 months.

🕑

Probation Periods

Statutory limits apply. A 1-year contract allows a max 2-month probation. A 3-year contract allows up to 6 months. Salary during probation cannot be less than 80% of the standard wage.

❌

Termination

"At-will" employment does not exist in China. You can only terminate an employee based on specific statutory grounds. Severance pay (usually 1 month per year of service) is standard.

🎓

Finding Talent

Use local platforms like Liepin, Zhaopin, or Boss Zhipin. LinkedIn is less effective for local drafters. English proficiency commands a premium in salary.
