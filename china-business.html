The Setup Process: Step-by-Step

Follow these exact, actionable steps to legally establish your business. For mainland China, the biggest strategic question is usually not the filing sequence alone—it is whether the ongoing cost of hosting the entity (office, compliance, payroll, and statutory contributions) fits your budget. The green timeline box under the WFOE tab shows where money sits before and after bank accounts. Steps 3–5 separate registered capital from operating burn; the Annual Financial Modeling Dashboard later on this page helps you quantify the largest variable: engineering payroll.

To hire talent in cities like Shanghai, Chengdu, or Shenzhen, you must form a Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise (WFOE). Follow this exact sequence. Registered capital is what you commit to contributing to the company; hosting cost is what you spend every year to keep the company alive and compliant—budget both before you file.

Where does the money go? (Follow this timeline)

  1. Steps 1–2: There is no WFOE bank account yet. Pay apostille fees, notary costs, and your own travel from personal funds or your offshore parent company. The commercial lease (Step 1) is often signed before the entity exists—deposits and early rent are commonly paid personally or by the foreign parent, then reimbursed by the WFOE after incorporation once accounts and chops exist (confirm the reimbursement path with your local agent and accountant).
  2. Steps 3–5: You are working on paper and in spreadsheets: fixed subscribed capital, roles, and articles. You do not wire registered capital into China at this stage—the company is not legally ready to receive equity in its own name.
  3. Steps 6–7: After the AMR issues the Business License, you carve chops. The entity exists but still has no usable corporate bank accounts until Step 8.
  4. Step 8: You open the company’s RMB basic account and the foreign-investment / capital account (exact names vary by bank). Accounts start empty (aside from any minimum the bank requires).
  5. Step 9: The foreign shareholder remits each tranche of registered capital from abroad into the designated account; the bank records paid-in capital (实缴) per SAFE rules. That is when your subscribed amount becomes cash inside China for the WFOE. Convert to RMB and move to the basic account for operations as your bank advises.
  6. Step 10: Tax, fapiao, and social insurance registration—then run payroll and statutory contributions from the RMB basic account, funded by that capital and/or customer revenue.
1

The Name & The Lease

  • Action: Pick a company name. The legal format is strict: [City] + [Your Name] + [Industry] + Co., Ltd. (e.g., Shanghai Acme CAD Design Co., Ltd.).
  • Action: Sign a lease for a commercial office. You cannot use a residential apartment.
  • CRITICAL: You must get a copy of the landlord's red "Property Ownership Certificate" with the lease, or the government will reject your application.
2

US Paperwork (Apostille)

  • Action: Take your US Passport (if applying as an individual) or your US LLC paperwork (if a company is the parent) to a local US Notary Public.
  • Action: Send the notarized document to your US state's Secretary of State to get an Apostille (an international certification).
  • Good News: As of recently, you no longer need to mail things to the Chinese Embassy. The Apostille is all China requires.
3

Pick Registered Capital (Strategic Amount)

  • What it is: Registered capital (注册资本) is the total amount shareholders subscribe to contribute to the company. It is listed on the Business License and caps shareholder liability in the limited-liability sense—it is not the same as your annual rent, payroll, or tax bill.
  • Law (2024 onward): For a WFOE as a limited liability company, shareholders must pay in the full subscribed amount within five years of establishment, per the articles of association (PRC Company Law revised, effective 1 July 2024). Schedule tranches inside that window; pre-2024 companies may have transition rules—confirm with counsel.
  • How to choose a number: (1) Minimum viable—many small services WFOEs use a modest figure (e.g. RMB 100k–500k or a foreign-currency equivalent) if there is no sector-specific minimum; (2) Business plan—enough paid-in capital over time to fund initial operations before revenue stabilizes (rent, salaries, software); (3) License / sector—some activities (e.g. certain construction, freight, or value-added telecom) require higher minimums or proof of funds; (4) Banking & partners—very low capital can complicate credit or large contracts; (5) Future repatriation—dividends and capital reduction follow rules tied to paid-in capital and profits; your accountant should align the amount with how you intend to move money in and out.
  • What it is not: It does not replace budgeting for employer social insurance and housing fund on top of salaries (those are separate statutory costs—see Step 4 and the dashboard below).
  • Money at this step: You are only deciding and documenting the amount in the articles. Do not send registered capital to China yet—the WFOE cannot lawfully receive your equity injection until after the license, chops, and bank accounts exist (see the green timeline above, and Steps 8–9).
4

Model Annual Hosting Costs (Operating Burn)

Before you lock articles and capital, stress-test whether you can afford to keep the entity running. This is what most founders actually mean by “cost of China”—not the filing fee, but the recurring tab. This step is still planning: you are not moving operating cash into a WFOE account until Step 9 (capital injection) and ongoing operations.

  • Who pays before the WFOE account exists: Early lease deposits, agents, and travel come from personal or offshore parent funds (Steps 1–2). After incorporation, the company may reimburse allowable pre-establishment costs—your agent must align this with local practice and tax rules.
  • Commercial real estate: Rent and property management for the licensed address (often your largest fixed line after headcount).
  • Corporate services: Bookkeeping, monthly/quarterly tax filing, annual audit (where required), agent for compliance—budget as a retainer, not a one-time fee.
  • Payroll & HR: Salaries plus employer “five insurances and one fund” (and any local surcharges). This is usually the dominant variable cost for a CAD/engineering shop—use the Annual Financial Modeling Dashboard on this page to compare cities and roles.
  • Banking & FX: Account maintenance, remittance fees, and documentation for inbound capital and outbound payments.
  • One-time setup: Chops, license-related fees, and initial IT (fapiao equipment) are small versus the first-year operating total—do not optimize registered capital based on chop cost alone.
  • Action: Build a simple 12-month cash-flow: sum the lines above and compare to your pay-in plan for registered capital plus expected revenue. Assume operating cash in China becomes available after Step 9 (and revenue later). If the model fails, reduce headcount or city tier before you commit a large subscribed capital figure.
5

Appoint Leadership Structure

  • Action: Name three mandatory roles: a Legal Representative (ultimate legal face of the company), an Executive Director (or a board), and a Supervisor.
  • Rule: The Supervisor cannot be the same person as the Director or Legal Rep—at least two distinct individuals on paper.
  • Tip: These names go into the articles filed with the registration authority; align them with who will actually sign contracts and bank mandates.
6

The Government Filing

  • Action: Submit your lease, apostilled passport, articles of association (capital, pay-in schedule, roles), and other company rules to the local Administration for Market Regulation (AMR).
  • Result: If approved, they hand you a physical paper document called the Business License (营业执照). Congratulations, your company officially exists!
7

Carve the "Chops" (Stamps)

  • Action: Take your new Business License to the Public Security Bureau (PSB).
  • Action: Hire a licensed vendor to carve your official company stamps, known as "Chops".
  • CRITICAL: In China, a red stamp on a contract is legally more powerful than your signature. Keep the Official Chop and Financial Chop locked in a safe. Whoever holds them controls the company.
8

Open Bank Accounts (Empty Accounts)

This is the first time the WFOE can hold money in its own name. Until this step, funds stayed with you or your offshore parent.

  • Action: With the Business License and chops, visit a major bank (e.g. ICBC, Bank of China, China Construction Bank). Expect KYC: purpose of the entity, ownership structure, and signatories.
  • Action: Open the RMB basic deposit account (基本户) —this is the main account for day-to-day RMB operations (salaries, rent, tax) and is usually the only account that can pay cash salaries.
  • Action: Open the foreign investor / capital account (names vary: e.g. capital account, FDI account) used to receive inbound foreign-currency investment from the shareholder abroad. The bank explains how it pairs with SAFE registration of your foreign-invested enterprise.
  • Important: Accounts may be opened with zero or minimal balance. You do not need to have already wired registered capital from the US—that is Step 9.
9

Remit Registered Capital (Paid-In Equity)

Now the shareholder sends money from abroad into the WFOE’s accounts. This is the legal moment your subscribed capital starts becoming paid-in (实缴) onshore.

  • Action: Work with your bank and corporate agent to prepare the foreign investment / capital contribution paperwork (forms and remittance purpose codes differ by bank; the wire must reference registered capital / 投资款 or the bank’s required wording—not a casual “transfer.”).
  • Action: The foreign parent (or you as individual investor) wires foreign currency (e.g. USD) to the WFOE’s designated inbound account. The bank records the amount toward your paid-in capital and SAFE filing; obtain FX settlement documents.
  • Action: Convert currency and move funds into the RMB basic account as needed for rent, salaries, and taxes—follow the bank’s process for capital-account settlement (资本金结汇); do not treat this like personal remittance.
  • Tranches: You may inject in multiple wires within your articles’ schedule, as long as you meet the five-year full pay-in rule (Step 3).
  • After this step: The WFOE has cash in China to fund operations. Steps 3–4 were budgets; Step 9 is when that equity actually arrives.
10

Tax & HR Ready

  • Action: Go to the local Tax Bureau to register and receive your "Fapiao" machine (the official government invoicing system).
  • Action: Register your company with the local Social Security Bureau. You are now legally ready to hire CAD drafters and pay their mandatory benefits from the RMB basic account (funded by Step 9 capital and/or operating revenue).

Because Hong Kong and Macau operate under separate legal systems, the setup process is entirely different and vastly simplified compared to the mainland WFOE.

🇭🇰

Hong Kong Setup

  1. Hire a Company Secretary: By law, you must hire a local HK individual or agency to act as your Company Secretary and provide a registered office address.
  2. Provide KYC Docs: Give the agency a copy of your US Passport and a recent utility bill (proof of address).
  3. File Online: The agency will file Form NNC1 (Incorporation Form) with the Companies Registry.
  4. Receive Certificate: The company is usually incorporated within 24 to 48 hours. No "chops" or complex government visits are required.
  5. Bank Account (The Hard Part): Opening a corporate bank account in HK is notoriously strict due to anti-money laundering laws. Expect this to take weeks and require an in-person visit or video interview.
🇲🇱

Macau Setup

  1. Name Approval: Submit an application to the Macau Commercial and Movable Property Registry (CRCBM) to reserve a company name.
  2. Draft Articles: Prepare the Memorandum and Articles of Association. Most foreign businesses form a "Limitada" (Lda.).
  3. Notary Signature: The founders must sign the incorporation documents in front of a Macanese Notary Public.
  4. Registration: File the notarized documents with the CRCBM.
  5. Tax Bureau: Submit a Declaration of Commencement of Operations to the Macau Finance Services Bureau. Total time: roughly 2 to 3 weeks.

Hainan Free Trade Port (Island)

Why Hainan matters in 2025–2027

The whole province operates as the Hainan Free Trade Port. As of late 2025, “special customs operations” apply island-wide: many goods imported from abroad enter with zero import duties, VAT, and consumption tax (with mainland-bound shipments generally taxed at the “second line” unless substantially transformed on-island). This is aimed at tourism, modern services, high-tech, healthcare, and logistics.

  • Corporate income tax: 15% for qualifying enterprises in encouraged industries with substantive operations (policy extended in phases, including through 31 Dec 2027 under current central notices).
  • Personal income tax: Eligible high-end and urgently-needed talent may cap the effective burden at 15% on qualifying Hainan-sourced income (rules for residency and recognition apply; current frameworks run through 31 Dec 2027).
