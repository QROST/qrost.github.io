Because Hong Kong and Macau operate under separate legal systems, the setup process is entirely different and vastly simplified compared to the mainland WFOE.

🇭🇰

Hong Kong Setup

  1. Hire a Company Secretary: By law, you must hire a local HK individual or agency to act as your Company Secretary and provide a registered office address.
  2. Provide KYC Docs: Give the agency a copy of your US Passport and a recent utility bill (proof of address).
  3. File Online: The agency will file Form NNC1 (Incorporation Form) with the Companies Registry.
  4. Receive Certificate: The company is usually incorporated within 24 to 48 hours. No "chops" or complex government visits are required.
  5. Bank Account (The Hard Part): Opening a corporate bank account in HK is notoriously strict due to anti-money laundering laws. Expect this to take weeks and require an in-person visit or video interview.
🇲🇱

Macau Setup

  1. Name Approval: Submit an application to the Macau Commercial and Movable Property Registry (CRCBM) to reserve a company name.
  2. Draft Articles: Prepare the Memorandum and Articles of Association. Most foreign businesses form a "Limitada" (Lda.).
  3. Notary Signature: The founders must sign the incorporation documents in front of a Macanese Notary Public.
  4. Registration: File the notarized documents with the CRCBM.
  5. Tax Bureau: Submit a Declaration of Commencement of Operations to the Macau Finance Services Bureau. Total time: roughly 2 to 3 weeks.

Hainan Free Trade Port (Island)

Show or hide Hainan Free Trade Port policy context Why Hainan matters in 2025–2027

The whole province operates as the Hainan Free Trade Port. As of late 2025, “special customs operations” apply island-wide: many goods imported from abroad enter with zero import duties, VAT, and consumption tax (with mainland-bound shipments generally taxed at the “second line” unless substantially transformed on-island). This is aimed at tourism, modern services, high-tech, healthcare, and logistics.

  • Corporate income tax: 15% for qualifying enterprises in encouraged industries with substantive operations (policy extended in phases, including through 31 Dec 2027 under current central notices).
  • Personal income tax: Eligible high-end and urgently-needed talent may cap the effective burden at 15% on qualifying Hainan-sourced income (rules for residency and recognition apply; current frameworks run through 31 Dec 2027).
